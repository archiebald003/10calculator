The framework

Three levers.
Every dollar of revenue.

All revenue that has ever existed came from one of three places: someone buying, buying more, or buying again. That's not a philosophy — it's accounting. The question is which levers you're actively managing, and which you've left on default.

1

Lever one

More Clients

Referral systems. Sharper positioning. Better offers at the door. New clients are necessary — but they're the most expensive growth you'll ever buy. Most businesses over-invest here and under-invest in the other two.

The reframe: A 10% increase in new clients costs marketing dollars. A 10% increase in the other two levers often costs almost nothing.

2

Lever two

Bigger Transactions

Bundling, premiums, add-ons, elevated service tiers. The customer who just said yes to you is the most likely person on earth to say yes again — immediately. Most businesses never make that second offer.

The reframe: Charging more isn't greed — it's often the truest expression of your actual value. Under-pricing is a form of dishonesty about what you deliver.

3

Lever three

More Frequent Buys

Re-engagement sequences. Seasonal offers. Loyalty programs. Maintenance reminders. Your existing clients already trust you — you don't have to earn them again. You just have to stay present and give them a reason.

The reframe: If a client hasn't returned, it's rarely because they disliked you. It's usually because no one asked them to.
