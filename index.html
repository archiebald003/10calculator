The framework

Three levers.
Every dollar of growth.

Within your existing operation, revenue comes from three places: who buys from you, how much they spend, and how often they come back. Most owners actively manage one. The 10-10-10 framework is about managing all three at the same time.

01

More Clients

Expand your reach, sharpen your message, build referral systems. This is the lever every business focuses on — and the most expensive one to move. It works. It's just not the whole story.

02

Bigger Transactions

A bundle, an upgrade, a premium tier, a complementary service. You've already earned their trust — now put it to work. Clients regularly spend more when given a genuinely valuable reason to.

03

More Frequent Buys

Bring existing clients back sooner. This is the cheapest growth you'll ever find — they already know you, trust you, and like you. A loyalty program, a well-timed offer, a simple follow-up can do it.

The real problem

Most businesses grow at a fraction of what the math allows.

Most business owners focus almost entirely on acquiring new customers — the most expensive lever to move. The other two levers sit untouched, and revenue stays flat as a result.

  • You spend heavily to acquire new customers while existing ones drift away silently
  • Most clients would spend more if presented with a relevant reason — few businesses consistently make that offer
  • Months pass between purchases that could happen in weeks
  • The result: linear growth at best, stagnation at worst
